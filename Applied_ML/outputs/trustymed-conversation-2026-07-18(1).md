what is alzheimer's

---

**TrustyMed** _(11:48 PM, confidence 85%)_

Alzheimer's disease (AD) is generally defined as follows:

*   **NINDS:** AD is an age-related, non-reversible brain disorder that develops over years. Initially, symptoms include memory loss and confusion, which can be mistaken for normal aging changes; however, they progress to behavior/personality changes, declining cognitive abilities (like decision-making and language skills), and problems recognizing family/friends. Eventually, it causes a severe loss of mental function due to the worsening breakdown and eventual death of connections between certain brain neurons [9742]. AD is one type of dementia characterized by cognitive and behavioral issues, and it is the most common cause of dementia for people aged 65 and older [9742]. Hallmarks associated with the disease include amyloid plaques (made from fragments of beta-amyloid peptide mixed with other proteins) and neurofibrillary tangles (abnormal clumps of tau protein found inside neurons) [9742].
*   **NIHSeniorHealth:** Alzheimers disease is a brain disease that slowly destroys memory, thinking skills, and eventually the ability to perform simple tasks; it gets worse over time and currently has no cure. It is cited as the most common cause of dementia in older people [254].
*   **MPlusHealthTopics:** AD is the most common form of dementia among older people (dementia being a brain disorder that affects daily activities) [1977]. It begins slowly, first affecting areas controlling thought, memory, and language. Symptoms worsen over time; individuals may have trouble recognizing family members, speaking, reading, or writing, forgetting basic self-care actions, and later developing anxiety, aggression, or wandering away from home, eventually requiring total care [1977]. AD usually begins after age 60, with the risk increasing with age. There is no treatment to stop the disease, though some drugs may temporarily help manage symptoms [1977].
